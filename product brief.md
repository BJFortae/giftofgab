# Product Brief

## Working Title: “WarmPath” (placeholder)

### One-Line Concept

A relationship-driven hiring platform that helps people bypass cold applications by activating trusted human networks to create warm introductions between candidates and hiring decision-makers.

---

# The Problem

The hiring process is broken in three major ways:

1. **Applications are commoditized**
  - AI will make resumes, cover letters, and applications increasingly identical.
  - Employers are overwhelmed by volume and keyword-matched noise.
  - Candidates disappear into ATS systems without human context.

1. **Trust matters more than credentials**
  - Most meaningful hiring still happens through relationships.
  - Companies trust referred candidates more than unknown applicants.
  - Candidates want advocates, not just application portals.

1. **Professional networks are underutilized**
  - Platforms like LinkedIn show who you know, but they do not operationalize relationships into structured hiring workflows.
  - Existing recruiting systems focus on jobs and resumes instead of trusted human connections.

---

# Vision

Build the relationship graph for hiring.

Instead of optimizing for applications, optimize for trusted introductions.

The platform becomes a human-powered talent marketplace where:

- people advocate for people,

- relationships become hiring infrastructure,

- and warm access becomes the primary hiring funnel.

---

# Core Insight

In an AI-driven world, human trust becomes the differentiator.

As AI automates:

- resume writing,

- portfolio creation,

- interview prep,

- and even skill assessments,

the scarce resource becomes:

> “Who knows you and is willing to vouch for you?”

This product turns personal relationships into structured hiring pathways.

---

# Product Concept

The platform connects three entities:

### 1. Talent

People looking for opportunities.

### 2. Connectors

Trusted intermediaries who know both sides.

### 3. Opportunity Owners

Hiring managers, founders, executives, recruiters, or advocates posting opportunities.

---

# Example Workflow

## Scenario

- Brandon knows a company called ResPyro is hiring a Chief Product Officer.

- Trent knows Brandon personally.

- Sarah is in Trent’s network and has deep product leadership experience.

Instead of Sarah blindly applying:

1. Brandon posts the opportunity.

1. The system identifies that Sarah is adjacent through Trent.

1. Sarah requests an introduction through Trent.

1. Trent can:
  - endorse Sarah,
  - make the introduction,
  - or decline.

1. Brandon receives a warm, trust-backed candidate introduction instead of another cold application.

## 2. Trust Is Earned

Not:

## 4. Human Advocacy Matters

Candidates should be able to gain momentum through:

- endorsements,

- referrals,

- recommendations,

- and trusted validators.

# B. Relationship Mapping Engine

The platform maps:

- first-degree connections,

- second-degree connections,

- trust proximity,

- industry overlap,

- and opportunity relevance.

This becomes the recommendation engine.

---

# C. Opportunity Posting

Anyone can post:

- jobs,

- advisory roles,

- consulting opportunities,

- project work,

- board positions,

- freelance engagements,

- or confidential searches.

Posters can identify:

- required skills,

- ideal background,

- industries,

- geography,

- compensation,

- urgency,

- and desired relationship proximity.

---

# D. Talent Identity Layer

Candidates create dynamic profiles including:

- skills,

- experiences,

- interests,

- goals,

- work preferences,

- industries,

- personality signals,

- and AI-generated capability summaries.

This should move beyond static resumes.

---

# E. Introduction Workflow

Core action flow:

1. Candidate sees opportunity through network

1. Candidate requests intro

1. Connector approves/rejects

1. Connector optionally adds context

1. Hiring side receives warm introduction

The connector becomes the bridge.

---

# Critical Product Challenges

## 1. Privacy

This is the biggest risk.

Questions:

- Who can see my network?

- Can someone infer my relationships?

- Do I control visibility?

- Can I opt out?

Potential solution:

- Invisible graph architecture

- Permission-based introductions

- No public exposure of contacts

---

# 2. Spam & Network Abuse

Risk:
People mass-request introductions.

Need:

- rate limiting,

- reputation systems,

- trust scoring,

- and social friction.

---

# 3. Trust Authenticity

Weak LinkedIn-style connections are not meaningful.

Need mechanisms to measure:

- real relationship depth,

- interaction frequency,

- and mutual trust.

---

# 4. Incentive Structure

Why would connectors participate?

Potential answers:

- social capital,

- reputation,

- recruiting incentives,

- referral fees,

- network influence,

- helping friends,

- executive status signaling.
